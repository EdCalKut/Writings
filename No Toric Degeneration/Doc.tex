We consider the surface defined by the polyhedral divisor 
\[
\{ \frac{1}{2} \} \otimes P_0 + \{0, \frac{1}{2}, 2 \} \otimes P_1 + \{0, \frac{1}{2} \} \otimes P_3
\]
with markings $\mathbb{Q}^\pm$. By contracting the curve corresponding to $2 \otimes P_1$ we get a $D_4$ singularity. So this is arising as a log terminal extraction from a non cyclic quotient singularity. This surface has one $\frac{1}{3}(1,1)$ and one $\frac{1}{8}(1,5)$ and $h^0(-K_X) = 4$.
